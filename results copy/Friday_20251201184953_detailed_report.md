### Historical Context and Significance
The study of the Moon's perigee and apogee (the farthest point in the Moon's orbit from Earth) has been significant in understanding the dynamics of the Earth-Moon system. These points have been used to model the gravitational interactions between the Earth and the Moon, predict phenomena such as tides and eclipses, and plan space missions. The variation in the Moon's distance from the Earth affects the strength of the gravitational pull, which in turn influences the height of tides on Earth. Historically, the perigee and apogee have been studied by astronomers and mathematicians to refine models of celestial mechanics. [Citation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perigee_and_apogee#Perigee)
